## Post 1 — AI and Cold Email: Tool vs. Replacement
*Source: linkedin.com/posts/jasondbay_ai-generated-cold-emails-activity-7161343851473870850-sqPo*

Jason tested AI-generated cold emails by having it write to prospects he was already in active conversations with — to see what he was missing. Key takeaways from his experiment and the discussion:

> "An extra set of hands, not an extra brain."

- AI should augment research and drafting, not replace the human judgment that makes emails feel real
- Using AI like a calculator: output quality depends entirely on input quality
- The emails "read naturally" but lacked the contextual nuance a rep with conversation history would include

**Takeaway**: AI as a starting point is legitimate. AI as the final sender is risky. The personalization layer requires human context that no prompt can fully capture.

## Post 2 — Mobile-Optimized Cold Email Framework
*Source: linkedin.com/posts/jasondbay_sales-prospecting-outbound-activity-7118206306963832833-XttI*

Jason shared data-backed email formatting rules:

> "Short and mobile-optimized emails see 83% more replies. Emails under 50 words get 60% more replies."

**The Basic Formula (every cold email must have)**:
1. **Reason** — why you're reaching out (specific, not generic)
2. **Problem** — a challenge the prospect actually faces
3. **Social Proof** — a relevant similar client or result
4. **CTA** — aligned with where the prospect is in their journey

**Formatting rules**:
- Under 75 words total
- 1–2 sentences per paragraph
- Write at a 5th-grade reading level
- Optimize for mobile — prospects read email on phones

**Example structure** (for a contact center software pitch):  
*"I noticed [Company] scaled to 400 reps last year. Most companies your size spend $2M+ on agent attrition annually. We helped [Similar Co] cut that by 40%. Worth a 15-min chat?"*

## Post 3 — Five Cold Calling Tactics That Actually Work
*Source: linkedin.com/posts/jasondbay_sales-prospecting-outbound-activity-7102259438702448641-nI04*

Jason shared a multi-touch cold calling playbook:

**1. Email Trigger Calls**  
Set up notifications when prospects open emails multiple times. Call them immediately — their attention is already on you.

**2. Video Follow-up**  
Send a Vidyard video, then call 10 minutes after they watch it. Opening line: "I was calling about the video I sent you about [topic]."

**3. Voicemail Redirects**  
Don't ask for callbacks. Instead redirect to another channel: "I just sent you an email with the subject line [insert line]." This drives cross-channel engagement.

**4. Assistant Outreach (Courtesy Call Technique)**  
Ask an assistant for help reaching the decision-maker. Genuine politeness and specificity ("I need 5 minutes of her time about X") outperforms gatekeeper bulldozing.

**5. Value Exchange Instead of Demo Requests**  
> "No one wants a demo. They want insights."  
Offer a specific insight from what's working with similar customers. Make a promise you can keep in 15 minutes.

## Post 4 — REPLY Method for Cold Outreach
*Source: LinkedIn / Blissful Prospecting content*

Jason's signature cold email framework:

| Letter | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| **R** | Results — share relevant results you've created for similar companies |
| **E** | Empathy — lead with a challenge or frustration they're experiencing |
| **P** | Personalization — something specific about their situation |
| **L** | Laser-focus — keep pitch under 15–30 seconds (email equivalent: 75 words) |
| **Y** | You — the message is about them, not you |

## Post 5 — 2024 Outbound Priorities
*Source: LinkedIn / Blissful Prospecting content*

Jason's condensed checklist for outbound in 2024–2025:

- **1 hour daily for outbound** — non-negotiable, protected time
- **Cut cold email copy in half** — from 150 words to 75 or less
- **Focus on impact over features** — what changes in their world, not what your product does
- **Work with SDRs** — find 2–3 common personalization patterns and build templates around them
- **A/B test everything** — subject lines, CTAs, email length
- **Niche down hard** — persona + industry specificity beats broad lists
- **Stay persistent** — 12–15 touches per prospect before stopping

> "70% of replies happen after the first email." — the follow-up strategy matters as much as the first touch.
